How to Use This Website

Struggle to get started? Here’s a step-by-step guide to help you get started:

🔍Homepage

The Homepage provides an overview of the website, a close look at the population of Australia and each of its territory and brief description of 17 socioeconomic outcomes.

Click the map to view the data of that area.

Click the arrow drop down of each outcome to view the description and target of the outcome.

Use the top menu to go to one of these sections:

  • Age & Health – See health data like age range and diseases
  • Education – Learn about school attendance and learning outcomes
  • Gap Analysis – Compare changes between 2016 and 2021

Each section gives you interactive data based on trusted national sources.

📊Understand the data

On the Age & Health/Education sub-page, you’ll see a Search Filter on the left to select the general data:

  • By sex: Male, Female
  • By status: Indigenous, Non-Indigenous, Not-Stated
  • By area: New South Wales, Tasmania, Victoria, etc.

On the right side of the page, continue to choose other options (Age Range, Diseases, Highest Year of School, etc.) based on the page you are selecting. After choosing all the categories, click the green button “Sort” to view the filtered data. When finished, you can click the red button “Clear all selection” and redo the process to view another sorted data.

💡What You Can Learn

You can use this website to:

  • See how health and education have changed over 5 years
  • Understand where progress is being made — and where it’s not
  • Support your work, study, or advocacy with real numbers

🌐Language and Accessibility

  • We use simple words and visual tools to help everyone understand
  • We aim to be respectful, inclusive, and easy to navigate

🤝Need Help?

If you’re unsure where to start:

  • Go to the Homepage for an overview
  • Visit the Contact Us page to ask a question
  • Explore the Vision or Personas pages to understand why this matters
